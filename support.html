Getting Started

Starting Your First Match

  1. Open Rally Score on your Apple Watch
  2. Tap "Start Match"
  3. Enter team names (e.g., "Team Red" and "Team Blue")
  4. Select your preferred scoring mode:
    • Side-Out: Traditional scoring (only serving team scores)
    • Rally: New 2025 rules (point on every rally)
  5. Choose point target: 11 (standard), 15, or 21
  6. Tap "Start Match"
üí° Pro Tip: If you're new to pickleball, start with Side-Out scoring at 11 points ‚Äì it's the most common format for recreational play.

Scoring Points

Once your match is underway, scoring is incredibly simple:

  • Tap "Team A Wins Rally" when Team A wins the rally
  • Tap "Team B Wins Rally" when Team B wins the rally
  • Rally Score automatically handles all service rotation
How It Works: In side-out scoring, only the serving team scores points. If the receiving team wins, they don't get a point but they do get the serve (side-out). Rally Score handles this automatically!

Using Undo

Made a mistake? No problem:

  1. Tap the "Undo" button
  2. The last rally will be reversed
  3. Score and server position return to previous state

Understanding Service Rotation

The 0-0-2 Start

According to USA Pickleball rules, matches always start with the "second server" (shown as 0-0-2). This means:

  • The first serving team starts with server #2
  • After one side-out, the opponent gets both servers (#1 and #2)
  • This ensures fairness in doubles play

Server Rotation (Doubles)

Rally Score automatically manages the rotation:

  1. Server 1: First server for the serving team
  2. Server 2: Second server (after Server 1 loses a rally)
  3. Side-Out: When Server 2 loses, serve passes to opponent

Even/Odd Positioning

Watch for the service court indicator:

  • Even (Right): Server stands on right when team score is even (0, 2, 4, 6...)
  • Odd (Left): Server stands on left when team score is odd (1, 3, 5, 7...)
üí° Pro Tip: You don't need to memorize these rules! Rally Score shows you exactly where the server should stand with visual indicators.

Match Management

Ending a Match

Games end automatically when win conditions are met:

  • A team reaches the target score (11, 15, or 21)
  • AND has a 2+ point lead (win-by-2 rule)

You can also manually end a match early:

  1. Tap "End Match"
  2. Confirm the action
  3. Match is saved to history with current score

Change Ends Prompt

Rally Score automatically prompts you to switch ends at the midpoint:

  • 11-point games: Switch at 6 points
  • 15-point games: Switch at 8 points
  • 21-point games: Switch at 11 points

Viewing Match History

  1. Scroll down on main watch screen
  2. Tap "History"
  3. See your last 10 completed matches
  4. Tap any match to view full details
  5. Swipe left on a match to delete it

Scoring Modes Explained

Side-Out Scoring (Traditional)

The classic pickleball scoring method:

  • Only the serving team can score points
  • Receiving team wins rally ‚Üí side-out (no point awarded)
  • Standard for recreational and competitive play
  • Most common format in clubs and tournaments

Rally Scoring (2025 Provisional Rules)

The new scoring method being tested:

  • Points awarded on every rally
  • Game-Point Freeze: Non-serving team cannot win on game point
  • Freeze-at-20 Variant: Alternative rec mode that freezes scoring at 20+ points
  • Points must be won on serve to complete game
  • Speeds up gameplay (fewer rallies needed)
üí° When to Use Rally Scoring: Try rally scoring for faster-paced games or when you want to experiment with the new 2025 rules. It's great for time-limited play sessions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Does Rally Score require internet?
No! Rally Score works completely offline. All match data is stored locally on your device.
Q: Can I use Rally Score for singles matches?
Yes! Rally Score supports both singles and doubles formats. The service rotation automatically adapts.
Q: How many matches can I save?
The current version stores your last 10 completed matches. Pro tier (coming soon) will offer unlimited history.
Q: What if the score seems wrong?
Use the "Undo" button to step back one rally. Remember: in side-out mode, only the serving team scores points. Check the "On Serve" indicator to see who's currently serving.
Q: Does Rally Score support tournament formats?
Currently, Rally Score is designed for single-game match play. Multi-game match support (best of 3, best of 5) is planned for future versions.
Q: Why isn't the Live Activity showing on my iPhone?
Ensure:
  • Your iPhone is running iOS 17.0 or later
  • Live Activities are enabled in Settings ‚Üí Notifications
  • Your iPhone and Apple Watch are paired
  • You've started a match on the watch (not iPhone)
Q: Can I customize team colors?
Custom color themes are planned for the Pro tier, coming soon. The current version uses default team colors (red and blue).
Q: What's the difference between side-out and rally scoring?
Side-out awards points only to the serving team. Rally scoring awards a point on every rally, but with special freeze rules at game point to ensure the winning team is on serve.
Q: What is the Stacking Coach feature?
The Stacking Coach (coming in v1.2) will show you and your partner exactly where to stand before each serve, with legal positioning guidance and post-serve movement suggestions for optimal team formations.
Q: Can I export my match data?
CSV export is planned for a future Pro tier update. This will allow you to export match statistics for analysis.

Troubleshooting

Match Won't Start

  • Ensure both team names are entered
  • Check that you've selected a scoring mode
  • Verify you've chosen a point target (11/15/21)
  • Try restarting the app if needed

Live Activity Not Showing

  • Check iOS version (must be 17.0+)
  • Verify Live Activities are enabled in Settings
  • Ensure devices are paired and connected
  • Try restarting both watch and iPhone

Haptics Not Working

  • Check that haptics aren't disabled in watch settings
  • Ensure watch isn't in Theater Mode
  • Verify watch is on wrist (haptics only work when worn)

App Crashes or Freezes

  • Force quit the app and restart
  • Restart your Apple Watch
  • Ensure you're running the latest watchOS version
  • If problem persists, contact support (see below)
